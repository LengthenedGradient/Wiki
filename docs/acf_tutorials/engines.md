## Fuel Types
You might be wondering, *what kind of fuel should I use in my tank?* It's really up to how you want your vehicle to perform.

### Petrol
* Used in high revving engines, they typically have a decent powerband with a fair amount of horsepower.
* While they do give a decent amount of power, they'll burn a lot of this precious go-go gas doing so.
* This is also highly flammable, and will most likely explode when shot with anything.

### Diesel
* Used in lower revving engines, these typically have a lot of torque to move those heavier vehicles.
* Since they are lower revving, they won't consume as much diesel, and they'll get you moving, if not very quickly.
* Since this fuel is compression based, it will only explode if hit with an explosive munition, yet it will leak if the tank is shot full of holes.

### Electric
* Elon Musk's dream of electric vehicles, electric engines are very heavy for their size, and so are the batteries.
* While being heavy, they will give you constant torque at all RPMs.
* The batteries won't leak or explode, and sometimes have been used as armor, just be careful to not rely on these when they do break.

## Engines
This will just cover what to expect from the engine shapes

### V-shape
* Decently revving engines, they'll get you going where you need to go.

### I-shape
* More decently revving engines, they'll also get you going where you need to go, just easier to fit while giving about half the power of a similar size V-shape engine.

### Boxer
* Another nice set of engines, they are flat if a bit thick, and give a steady amount of power.

### Radial
* Typically found aircraft, these have decent torque and horsepower at the downside of having a rather high powerband and being wonky to fit.

### Rotary (Wankel)
* Usually found in small fast cars, these give amazing power to weight ratio for their size, while burning all of your fuel in one fell swoop.

### Turbine
* These fuel sucking beasts will give you boatloads of torque and power, but they are a bit slow to react to changes in throttle.

### Electric
* As mentioned above, these will give you a constant amount of torque while consuming a static amount of electricity.
